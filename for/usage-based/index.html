The blind spot

The buyer you can't see.

In a usage-based business, the account that matters most looks like nothing on day one. A developer evaluates you inside Claude or Cursor, signs up as alex@gmail.com, never opens your chat, never books a demo, and starts sending. By the time the invoice is big enough for sales to notice, the decision was made weeks ago — and you had no seat at the table.

Domain enrichment returns nothing: there's no company behind a Gmail. Website de-anonymisation returns nothing: he was never on your website. The signal that he's becoming your next six-figure account isn't on any surface those tools watch. It's already inside your product.

Why the tools you've tried miss him

They're watching the wrong surface.

Visitor-identification platforms watch your website and enrich on firmographics — funding rounds, hiring spikes, job changes — bought from the same data providers everyone uses. They fire on website visitors. The developer scaling your API was never a visitor. When they say "product usage," they mean used our chat widget — not tripled send volume this week.

The account that matters is invisible to anything that starts at the website. See how this differs from a website de-anonymisation tool in LUPO vs Warmly.

The mechanism

From a usage signal to a named, qualified account.

Four steps, in this order — the order is the point.

Step 1 · Fraud gate first

Filter fraud before you score value.

A spike in usage is ambiguous: your next whale, or a fraudster pumping traffic. LUPO filters fraud before it scores value — OTP-conversion collapse, suspicious carrier and geo patterns, the absence of trust acts. Score value before you filter fraud and you put fraudsters at the top of your sales queue. The gate runs first.

Step 2 · Score the behaviour, not the name

Importance, computed independently of identity.

On the accounts that pass, LUPO scores usage trajectory — send-volume velocity and acceleration, top-up size and cadence, production-versus-test ratio, time-to-first-value, and trust acts like a payment method added or a 10DLC brand registered. An anonymous Gmail account accelerating into production ranks at the top of the queue, marked identity pending. It is never dropped for missing a company name.

Step 3 · Resolve identity three ways

Where databases fail, the conversation works.

Where the email resolves, LUPO enriches it. Where enrichment is structurally dead — the freemail majority — LUPO ranks the account on behaviour alone and, for the high-value ones, does what a sharp SDR would: opens a conversation with the usage context in hand. "You registered a 10DLC brand yesterday and tripled your send volume — what are you building?" The developer tells you who he is. That works exactly where the databases fail.

Step 4 · Write the verdict to your CRM

Not a score. A story your rep can act on.

"Freemail account, send volume up 340% week-over-week, second accelerating week, two top-ups ($500 → $2,000), 10DLC brand registered yesterday, fraud-clear, OTP 94%. Said in chat they're integrating dispatch alerts for Acme Logistics. Likely scaling production buyer."
The moat

One timeline. What he said, and what he did.

LUPO puts the conversation and the consumption on a single per-account timeline — what the developer told your chat, and what he's doing inside your product — in one place, written to the CRM your reps already live in.

Usage signals commoditise. Enrichment is rented. The conversation at your front door is yours: first-party, and impossible to buy. That fused record is the thing no website tool and no data platform can assemble — because each owns only half of it.

It acts

LUPO doesn't hand you a screen to monitor. It scores, it decides, and it acts — opening the conversation, qualifying, booking, and writing the verdict to the CRM. The intelligence exists to drive an action, not to populate a chart you'll check on Friday.

Common questions

Our usage data isn't in one clean place. Does that matter?

It doesn't need to be. LUPO reads a signal you already own — a billing webhook, a CDP tap, or a read-only warehouse share — at least privilege. Never raw logs.

How is this different from Warmly or Common Room?

They watch your website and score firmographics. LUPO watches your product's consumption stream and fuses it with the conversation. The developer scaling your API is off-website, so he's invisible to anything that starts there. Full comparison: LUPO vs Warmly.

What about GDPR and person-level identity?

Company-level everywhere; person-level US-only, by design; EU and UK fenced. Identity is stitched deterministically first; probabilistic matching is caged and never moves a score or fires a message on its own.

How fast can we be live on our own funnel?

Fully live in 2 to 4 weeks. Every engagement starts with a 30-day founder-led pilot against success criteria you agree up front.

Which CRM do you write to?

Any. HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, Attio natively; anything else by webhook.
