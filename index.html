For sales leaders

The questions VP Sales and RevOps actually ask.

Plain answers. No magic. LUPO is an inbound qualification and routing layer, not a chatbot and not a replacement for your reps.

Will LUPO annoy senior buyers?

No. LUPO is channel-aware. A senior enterprise buyer who submits a form receives a thoughtful email with a booking link — not an unexpected robot call. Phone callbacks fire when the channel and policy make sense (consumer forms, inbound calls, or chats where the visitor has explicitly asked to be called).

Is this just a chatbot?

No. LUPO is an inbound qualification layer across calls, web forms, chat handoffs, and email. Chat is one intake channel of four. LUPO does not replace your chat widget — your existing chatbot (Intercom Fin, Drift, Qualified, custom) hands off to LUPO when a visitor is ready.

Does LUPO replace SDRs?

No. LUPO handles the inbound triage and qualification work that punishes responsive sales teams. Human SDRs and AEs still own complex discovery, negotiation, and relationship-building. LUPO buys them time by filtering noise and routing only qualified buyers.

What happens when LUPO is unsure?

It escalates instead of guessing. Depending on your policy, an uncertain or high-value lead is held for human approval, routed to a named rep, or asked a clarifying question before LUPO commits to an action. LUPO never forces a booking it is not confident about.

What happens to vendors and support requests?

Vendor pitches are politely declined and logged — never routed to sales. Existing-customer support requests are routed to the right team with the original conversation attached. AEs see only the buyers worth their time.

Can it work with our CRM and calendar?

Today: HubSpot, Slack, Google Calendar, and Resend are live; Vapi powers the voice path. Any chatbot that can post a webhook can hand off to LUPO. Microsoft 365 / Outlook calendar is in onboarding. Salesforce CRM sync is on the roadmap. We do not publish integrations we have not shipped.
