LUPO + Slack

LUPO alerts your team in Slack the second a lead is qualified or a meeting books, with a one-click link to full context.

What it does

LUPO is the AI inbound SDR across web form, email, chat, and phone. When it finds a real buyer, your reps hear about it where they already work.

The alert

  • Fires the second a lead is qualified or a meeting books
  • Carries the essentials: who, company, channel, intent score
  • Noise never pings anyone; filtered leads are logged, not broadcast

One click to context

  • Full transcript of the conversation
  • Enrichment: firmographics, technographics, decision-maker title
  • Intent score with the triggers behind it
  • Reps act without leaving Slack

How it works for your team

A buyer calls at 4:55pm on a Friday. LUPO answers, qualifies, and books on the call. The alert lands in Slack before the call ends: name, company, intent score, meeting time. One click to transcript and enrichment.

What does not happen matters just as much. Vendors, tire-kickers, support requests, and wrong-fit leads are filtered and logged in the CRM with their outcome. Slack stays quiet unless there is a real buyer to act on.

Setup

Slack is connected during pilot onboarding, OAuth-based, with channels and routing configured hand-in-hand with your team. Alerts are live inside the 2 to 4 week rollout.

Common questions

Which events trigger a Slack alert?

LUPO alerts the second a lead is qualified or a meeting books. Filtered noise such as vendors, tire-kickers, support requests, and wrong-fit leads never pings anyone. Alert rules are configured during onboarding to match how your team works.

Can alerts go to specific channels or reps?

Yes. Routing is configured during onboarding to match how your team works, hand-in-hand with your team during the pilot.

Does the Slack alert replace the CRM record?

No. Your CRM stays the system of record. Every qualified contact, call summary, transcript link, booking, qualification outcome, and intent score is written into HubSpot or Salesforce automatically. The Slack alert is the fast path to it, with a one-click link to full context.
