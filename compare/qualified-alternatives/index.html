Qualified Alternatives for HubSpot Teams

Qualified became a Salesforce company in April 2026, and its roadmap points at Salesforce-based teams. If your team runs HubSpot, here are the six alternatives worth evaluating, and the job each one is best at.

The short answer

For live inbound qualification across web form, email, chat, and phone, the closest like-for-like is LUPO, our own product, so judge that claim accordingly; the nearest rival is Spara. For reactivating a large lead database, Conversica. For support conversations, Intercom Fin. Drift carries a publicly reported sunset. If the real gap is cold pipeline, the answer is outbound: 11x.

Why teams look for an alternative

Three reasons come up. First, Qualified is designed for Salesforce-based revenue teams; on HubSpot you are outside the core design. Second, pricing is custom annual contracts. Third, Salesforce completed its acquisition of Qualified on April 1, 2026, deepening the coupling. For a HubSpot-centered team, the wrong direction of travel. Full head-to-head: LUPO vs Qualified.

The alternatives

1. LUPO

LUPO is our product, so judge this entry accordingly. LUPO is the AI inbound SDR: one agentic brain across web form, email, chat, and phone. It engages the moment a visitor lands, filters the noise, enriches every lead, scores buying intent, books real buyers into reps' calendars, and syncs everything to the CRM.

HubSpot is a live, native integration, alongside Salesforce, Slack, Google Calendar, and Microsoft 365 / Outlook. High-intent forms get a callback inside sixty seconds; phone is answered on the second ring, 24/7. Pricing is 3,000 to 15,000 USD per month, stated upfront, with a 30-day founder-led pilot.

2. Spara

Spara is the closest like-for-like alternative on this list. Launched in September 2025 with 15 million USD in seed funding, it deploys voice, email, chat, and text AI agents, plus AI-led product demos, to engage and qualify inbound in real time; customers include Rho, MarketMan, Jericho Security, and TinyMCE.

The two compete head-on at the engagement layer. LUPO then adds the layers behind the conversation: enrichment on every lead, buying-intent scoring, phone answered on the second ring, sixty-second callbacks on high-intent forms. Spara's pricing is publicly listed from 2,100 USD per month, billed annually. Full comparison: LUPO vs Spara.

3. Conversica

Conversica's Revenue Digital Assistants run persistent two-way follow-up over email, SMS, and chat; its strength is reactivating large lead databases. Integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Marketo; publicly reported from 2,999 USD per month on annual contracts. The right pick when the job is a dormant database, not live inbound. Full comparison: LUPO vs Conversica.

4. Intercom Fin

Fin is Intercom's AI agent for customer service, built to resolve support conversations. It is publicly listed at 0.99 USD per resolution. If the conversations you want automated are support rather than sales, Fin is the fit, and it pairs cleanly with a sales-side agent: Intercom chat can hand sales-intent conversations to LUPO. Full comparison: LUPO vs Intercom Fin.

5. Drift

Drift pioneered conversational marketing, and one caveat now dominates: Salesloft acquired Drift in 2024, and in March 2026 Clari and Salesloft announced a gradual sunset, with 1mind named as successor, as publicly reported. Teams in that ecosystem may have a transition path; as a new purchase, most teams look elsewhere. Full comparison: LUPO vs Drift.

6. 11x

If inbound is thin and the real gap is cold outreach, the answer is outbound. 11x's digital worker Alice runs prospecting, outreach, replies, and booking over email and LinkedIn; publicly reported from about 5,000 USD per month, with first-year minimums around 50,000 to 60,000 USD. Full comparison: LUPO vs 11x.
